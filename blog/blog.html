Listening (a long forgotten trait)


One of the most sincere form of respect is to actually listening what another has to say

The most gracious thing to offer is to listen to what the other person is saying, as it provides comfort to the speaker and is a source of knowledge for the listener, but do we listen nowadays? In this era of globalization we listen to reply rather than to understand. So what difference does that make? Many individuals argue that all you need to do is to reply to the other guy, besides there is no other option, moreover if you see the flipside of the story, the speaker not always wants a reply or a solution he or she just want to shout out their woes to someone he or she trusts. According to Umair Jalliawla “If you start a business of listening to people, you’ll be rich in the shortest span of time”





Social & Personal Relationships


No relationship is a waste of time. If it didn’t bring you what you want, it taught you what you want

Over the years people’s psyche and perception about relationships have changed, back then when our parents were young, they use to value relationships be it a family or social relationships. According to some old people, in their time the most important aspects of society was a mutual respect, a cognitive behavior that never led to cognitive dissonance but to a cognitive development, where friends became brothers and family became the indispensable element of one’s life.





A Family Life


A family is the one of the most important blessing bestowed upon mankind, if it wasn’t we would be more like an animals, animals who herd around each other but not with their family. For many of us friends and late night gatherings are far more important than our families, we are a generation who prefer hanging out with strangers but not with our brothers, we are a generation that prefers hanging out with our friends but not with our parents. In the name of urbanization we have forgotten our customs and traditions, the most important aspects of which was a family life.
