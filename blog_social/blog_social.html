Social & Personal Relationships



No relationship is a waste of time. If it didn’t bring you what you want, it taught you what you want

Over the years people’s psyche and perception about relationships have changed, back then when our parents were young, they use to value relationships be it a family or social relationships. According to some old people, in their time the most important aspects of society was a mutual respect, a cognitive behavior that never led to cognitive dissonance but to a cognitive development, where friends became brothers and family became the indispensable element of one’s life.
In this era of proliferation the human span of attention has been reduced to a minimum level, the introduction of mobile technologies have created ease in every step of our life, be it connecting with the people, or anything for that matter, But have we lost something? Indeed we lost ourselves in this era of social media. Relationships are just for the sake of some meanings, if I’m receiving some sort of benefit out of relationship then only I’ll be interested in having the relationship otherwise, nothing much it was just a bad phase of life.
It’s not even been a century and our perception towards relationships have been completely transformed from the most valuable thing to most undesirable element of the society. Children are running from their relatives, the family gatherings that were previously very charming and soothing are now the most boring thing in the entire universe. Listening to the stories of granny is now considered to be the waste of time, well back then it was the most adventurous thing.
So what happened? Are we too busy to manage time for relationships? Have we become so mean to cater for our own benefits? Or are we too busy on focusing on the worldly things that won’t last long, or the blindness have become so common that we’re not able to see what’s for long term or what is for short term.
