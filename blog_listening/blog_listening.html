Listening (a long forgotten trait)



One of the most sincere form of respect is to actually listening what another has to say

The most gracious thing to offer is to listen to what the other person is saying, as it provides comfort to the speaker and is a source of knowledge for the listener, but do we listen nowadays? In this era of globalization we listen to reply rather than to understand. So what difference does that make? Many individuals argue that all you need to do is to reply to the other guy, besides there is no other option, moreover if you see the flipside of the story, the speaker not always wants a reply or a solution he or she just want to shout out their woes to someone he or she trusts. According to Umair Jalliawla “If you start a business of listening to people, you’ll be rich in the shortest span of time”
So why do we don’t listen? Be it a class lecture, or someone sharing his or her knowledge, listening is the least we do in a day, and that’s why our generation is always in some sort of conflict or an argument with one another, we don’t listen we hear and forget what the other person said. For what I believe, the world is a beautiful place and we all have a different perspective of it, or in other words every being has his or her vision of the world and the only way to get familiar with it is to listen to people, what they think? What they believe? What they see? Leading our lives according to our own experiences and knowledge is a good thing but the better thing is to listen to people what they have to say about a particular thing and then to decide what is the best.
A teacher won’t be a teacher if he wouldn’t have listened, a doctor is not able to cure unless he is listens to the problem, and a consultant won’t be able to guide you unless he listens.
So why don’t we listen? Why do we hear things out and base our judgment on that hearing, perhaps we are too busy in our own lives that we forget that someone has to say something.
